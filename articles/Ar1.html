Salary Hike Shock, Naveen Opts Out, But Why Did BJD Help BJP Push a 3x Pay Jump?

Published on December 13, 2025

Follow Us:

X Facebook Instagram Threads

On December 13, 2025, Odisha's former Chief Minister and Leader of Opposition, Naveen Patnaik, announced he would forgo his recently hiked monthly salary and allowances, which rose from about Rs 1.1 lakh to Rs 3.62 lakh. He requested that the enhanced amount be redirected for the welfare of the poor.

The announcement came amid public criticism of the Odisha Legislative Assembly Members’ Salary, Allowances and Pension (Amendment) Bill, 2025. Salary hikes for legislators are not unusual, but a nearly threefold increase at one stroke triggered widespread debate. Patnaik's decision also echoed his family's 2015 donation of the ancestral Anand Bhavan property for public use, reinforcing a tradition linked to his late father, Biju Patnaik.

A Rare Moment of Unanimity

The salary hike bill was passed unanimously on December 9 and 10, 2025, with support from the BJP, BJD, Congress, and independents. It raised MLA pay from around Rs 1.11 lakh to about Rs 3.45 lakh per month, placing Odisha MLAs among the highest paid in India. For the Leader of Opposition, the total monthly package touched roughly Rs 3.62 lakh, along with retrospective arrears from June 5, 2024.

While pay revisions are often defended due to inflation and rising costs, the scale of this decision stood out. A near 3x jump, approved without visible resistance inside the Assembly, sharpened public anger.

Why BJD Stayed On Board

BJD's support was a political choice, not a legal requirement. Party leaders said the revision was a long pending demand since 2007, with salaries unchanged since 2017 despite rising medical and constituency expenses. Senior BJD leader and Opposition Chief Whip Pramila Mallik publicly justified the hike, pointing to the needs of ageing and unwell former MLAs and comparisons with states like Telangana and Maharashtra.

With the BJP holding 78 of the 147 seats, BJD opposition would not have stopped the bill. Yet by backing it, BJD avoided confrontation and helped create rare cross party consensus.

Shared Responsibility, Personal Opt Out

The only clear dissent came from CPI(M) MLA Laxman Munda, who criticised the hike during committee discussions but was absent during the final vote due to a protest outside the House.

Had BJD opposed the bill, the BJP government would have stood alone and faced the full force of public anger. That pressure could have pushed the government to reconsider the scale or timing of the salary hike. Instead, unanimous support from all major parties gave the decision political cover and made the steep increase easier to implement.

Naveen Patnaik's decision to give up his enhanced salary is an individual ethical choice, not party policy. It applies only to him and does not alter the benefits that other MLAs will receive under the law.

What It Signals

Patnaik's move received praise for its moral intent and also some criticism for not addressing his party's role in supporting the bill. The episode highlights a clear divide between personal restraint and collective responsibility. Individual sacrifice can send a strong message, but public trust ultimately depends on the choices political parties make inside the Assembly.
