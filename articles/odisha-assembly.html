Odisha Assembly Elections 2024 - Comprehensive Expenditure Analysis

Published on December 1, 2025

The 2024 Odisha Assembly election expenditure affidavits reveal how political parties financed and executed their campaigns, offering an unprecedented look into spending patterns across BJD, BJP, INC, CPI(M), and others. The data exposes sharp contrasts in campaign styles, from high-intensity public meetings to minimal digital outreach, while highlighting the dominance of party-funded machinery, significant self-financing gaps, and emerging transparency concerns in election spending.

1. Executive Summary

The 2024 Odisha Assembly election expenditure affidavits of 147 elected MLAs show:

Average spending: ₹26.22 lakh (66 percent of the ₹40 lakh limit)

BJD MLAs: highest average spending among major parties

CPI(M) MLA: highest utilisation (91.4 percent)

INC MLAs: lowest average spending, and the highest self-funding dependency

Public meetings dominate costs, virtual campaigns remain negligible (less than 1 percent)

Party funding contributes 84 percent of all MLA financing, indicating strong party-controlled campaign structures.

7. Compliance Gaps

Late uploading of statements on CEO website

Missing totals, ADR had to compute from sub-totals

Illegible, overwritten entries

Petition window of 45 days is too narrow if uploads are delayed

8. Recommendations

Mandatory upload within 72 hours

Auto-validation digital forms, no blanks allowed

Extend petition window or enforce timely uploads

Provide machine-readable formats (CSV or JSON)

Introduce random audits

Public dashboard for expenditure comparison

9. Conclusion

The Odisha 2024 MLA expenditure dataset reveals:

BJD and BJP run structured, party-funded campaigns

INC MLAs spend much less and rely on self-funding

CPI(M)'s sole MLA dramatically outspends INC averages

Public meetings comprise the largest cost component, not media or digital outreach

Star campaigner spending is overwhelmingly a BJD, BJP domain

END NOTE

This report is based exclusively on expenditure affidavits filed by MLAs and uploaded on the CEO Odisha website, with analysis by ADR and Odisha Election Watch.

It comprehensively covers expenditure patterns of BJD, BJP, INC, CPI(M), and Independent MLAs, with thematic and constituency-wise detail.
