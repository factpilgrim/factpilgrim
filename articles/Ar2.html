After 25 deaths in Goa, are Bhubaneswar pubs a disaster waiting to happen?

Published on December 13, 2025

Follow Us:

X Facebook Instagram Threads

The deadly nightclub fire in Goa that killed 25 people has exposed a worrying reality closer home. In Bhubaneswar, more than 80 standalone bars and restaurants with bar facilities are operating without any formal fire safety clearance, even as hundreds of people visit them every day.

What the law allows and what it ignores

Under the Odisha Fire Prevention and Fire Safety Rules, 2017, only certain categories of buildings are required to obtain a fire safety certificate. The criteria depend on factors like building height and number of floors. Standalone bars, pubs and smaller restaurants usually do not meet these specifications, which means the law does not strictly compel them to seek certification.

The numbers tell their own story

Sources in the Excise department say Bhubaneswar has about 58 hotels and 82 restaurants with bars. Fire safety certificates have been issued to most hotels, but smaller pubs and restaurants largely remain outside the fire services jurisdiction. Many of these establishments claim to have safety measures, but without formal clearance, there is no uniform standard or verification.

Two clearances, but little clarity

Such commercial premises can either obtain a fire safety certificate under the rules or apply for a no objection certificate. Odisha Fire and Emergency Service directly oversees hotels, while smaller clubs, pubs and restaurants fall through regulatory gaps. Most operate with a trade licence from the Bhubaneswar Municipal Corporation, without mandatory fire clearance.

Inspections after public pressure

After the Goa incident, fire services have decided to closely examine similar establishments in Bhubaneswar and Cuttack. Inspections are planned from December 13 onwards to check if bars and pubs have a valid fire safety no objection certificate and basic facilities like emergency exits, fire detection systems, sprinklers and extinguishers.

Authorities acknowledge the gap

DG Fire Services Sudhansu Sarangi has directed officers to identify hotels that lack fire safety certificates and inspect clubs and pubs as well. If shortcomings are found, agencies like the Bhubaneswar Municipal Corporation and the Commissionerate Police will be informed. A one page advisory on fire safety lapses will also be shared with owners and the Excise department.

The larger question of accountability

There are about 120 bars and pubs across Bhubaneswar and Cuttack. The inspection drive is expected to conclude by month end. But the bigger issue remains, should public safety depend on technical definitions in rules, or on the real risk to human life.

Until regulations catch up with ground realities, the comfort of legality may continue to mask serious danger.
