Rupee Hits All-Time Low of ₹89.41, Sparks Political Storm and Social Media Outrage

Published on December 2, 2025

The Indian rupee plunged to a historic low of ₹89.41 against the US dollar on November 27 and later recovered slightly to ₹89.22 to ₹89.23. The sharp fall, driven by stalled India-US trade talks, heavy foreign investor outflows, and initially limited Reserve Bank of India intervention, has ignited fierce political attacks and widespread public anger on social media. 

Congress Revives Modi's 2013 Remarks

Opposition parties wasted no time in targeting the Narendra Modi government. The Congress party shared a widely circulated video on X with the caption "Modi's Biggest Achievement: Rupee To Soon Hit A Century Against Dollar". Senior leader Jairam Ramesh posted a 2013 clip of Modi criticising the UPA government for a weaker rupee and asked whether the Prime Minister remembered his own words. 

TMC Blames Foreign Policy Missteps

Trinamool Congress blamed "PR stunts and vanity photo-ops" in foreign policy, pointing to 25 percent US tariffs on Indian goods that are higher than those on China or Japan. 

BJP Silent on Record Low

The Bharatiya Janata Party stayed largely silent on the November 27 low itself. Senior leader Amit Malviya highlighted data showing the rupee depreciated only 3.68 percent in the past year, far less than the Japanese yen or South Korean won, and described it as one of the most stable currencies globally under the current administration. 

Social Media Turns Hostile With Memes and Anger

On X, hashtags such as #RupeeVsDollar, #NewIndiaFail and #ModiEconomyCrash surged past 150,000 mentions within 48 hours. Real-time sentiment analysis of high-engagement posts showed 72 percent negative, 18 percent neutral and only 10 percent positive sentiment. Users expressed fury over rising import costs, with viral posts lamenting pricier fuel, electronics and foreign education. Memes depicting the rupee on its "last journey" or as a sinking ship captained by a "Vishwa Guru" gathered thousands of likes and reposts. 

Opposition Narrative Dominates Online

Opposition supporters dominated the conversation, contrasting Modi's 2013 criticism of the UPA with today's record lows. Pro-government accounts countered that annual depreciation remains modest compared with the 2013 crisis, but such posts were heavily outnumbered and often ratioed. 

Economists Warn of Continued Pressure

Economists on the platform attributed the slide to a stronger US dollar, persistent trade deficits and foreign fund exits. Many explained the Reserve Bank's strategy of managed depreciation while warning of continued pressure if trade negotiations with the United States remain deadlocked.

By Monday evening the rupee had stabilised around ₹89.23 after fresh RBI dollar sales, yet online anger showed no signs of cooling. With the next Monetary Policy Committee meeting approaching, millions of X users turned the currency fall into a real-time referendum on economic management, middle-class purchasing power and the government's global standing.
