Odisha Police Register FIR Against YouTuber Shubhankar Mishra for Spreading False Story on Jagannath Temple

Published on December 2, 2025

Follow Us:

X Facebook Instagram Threads

The Odisha Police have registered an FIR against popular YouTuber and journalist Shubhankar Mishra at the Singhadwar Police Station in Puri for allegedly spreading misinformation and hurting the religious sentiments of devotees of Lord Jagannath.

The case, filed under Section 295A of the Indian Penal Code, follows a complaint submitted by Gourahari Pradhan, a senior servitor of the Jagannath Temple. Pradhan accused Mishra of circulating a fabricated narrative claiming that Goddess Radha Rani cursed the Jagannath Temple after being denied entry. According to the video shared by Mishra, the supposed “curse” leads to failed relationships among unmarried couples who visit the shrine.

Temple scholars and officials have refuted the claim, clarifying that no such account exists in any authoritative text, including the Skanda Purana, the central scripture associated with Jagannath traditions.

In response to the controversy, the Shree Jagannath Temple Administration (SJTA) formed an inquiry committee on November 29 to examine the impact of the viral video. Puri District Collector Dibyajyoti Parida stated that the dissemination of false narratives about Jagannath culture would not be tolerated.

Puri Superintendent of Police Prateek Singh confirmed that the FIR has been registered and that an investigation is underway. Police teams are currently collecting audio and video materials from Mishra’s social media channels for verification.

Shubhankar Mishra, who hails from Gonda, Uttar Pradesh, and is presently based in Noida, has not issued any public clarification regarding the allegations.

The development has sparked widespread reaction online, with many social media users applauding the swift action taken to uphold the sanctity of the revered Jagannath Temple.
