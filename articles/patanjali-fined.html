Patanjali Fined for Substandard Ghee as Outrage Grows Over Delayed Action and Lenient Penalty

Published on November 30, 2025

Follow Us

X (Twitter) Facebook Instagram Threads

A Uttarakhand court on November 27, 2025, fined Patanjali Ayurved ₹1 lakh after a 2020 sample of its desi cow ghee failed quality tests in two government labs. The ghee was declared substandard, and unfit for human consumption due to low Reichert-Meissl value, indicating possible adulteration with vegetable fats. The distributor and retailer were fined ₹25,000 and ₹15,000 respectively, bringing the total penalty to ₹1.4 lakh.

No recall was ordered during the five year investigation period, allowing the product to remain on shelves. Patanjali has rejected the verdict, called the labs unaccredited, and said the test parameters are outdated. The company plans to appeal, stating that any variation is natural, and that no health harm was proven.

The case has triggered widespread anger over the small fine, relative to the multi-crore ghee sales of Patanjali, the long delay in justice, and recurring quality issues (including earlier honey adulteration scandals). Many users are calling for stricter penalties, nationwide re testing, and boycotts, while others question regulatory leniency. The topic continues to trend heavily in India.
