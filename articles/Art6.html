Delhi CM stuns summit with pollution theory while Delhi battles missing data

Published on December 10, 2025

Follow Us:

X Facebook Instagram Threads
At a recent media summit in Delhi, Chief Minister Rekha Gupta made several claims about how air pollution is measured. She described the Air Quality Index as a simple measure like temperature, and suggested that it can be checked from any device at any place. Experts say AQI is a complex calculation, and it depends on strict scientific rules and specialised equipment.

Gupta was also asked about reports that water was being sprayed near air monitoring stations, which can reduce dust and make AQI numbers appear better. She did not clearly deny the practice, and instead said that such spraying helps improve the soil and supports pollution control efforts.

The discussion came at a time when several AQI stations in Delhi have shown missing or incomplete data. Independent observers say that gaps in pollution data are most common during the worst smog days, and this raises questions about transparency. Some stations are also placed in locations that do not meet technical norms, such as behind walls or under trees, which may affect readings.

Gupta claimed that the government has maintained stable air quality even after major festivals, but ground reports show continued smog and poor visibility. Data for key days is also missing, which makes verification difficult. She also made comments on the Yamuna cleanup, and critics say these statements do not match the condition of the river.

Environmental groups and researchers say that accurate and continuous data is essential for public health and policy planning. They warn that any attempt to oversimplify pollution science, or any gap in data, can mislead citizens and weaken efforts to clean the air.
