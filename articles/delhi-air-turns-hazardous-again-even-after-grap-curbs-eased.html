Delhi Air Turns Hazardous Again Even After GRAP Curbs Eased

Nov 27, 2025 – 10:59 am Health

Delhi’s air quality continues to remain in the hazardous range, even as authorities eased restrictions by rolling back GRAP-III measures on November 26. The decision came after the city’s overall AQI improved to 327, placed in the Very Poor category, prompting a downgrade to the milder GRAP-II rules. This relaxation allows halted construction activities to resume, and restores full physical attendance in offices.

Despite the official improvement, several neighbourhoods are still recording AQI levels close to 500, signalling severe health risks. Adding to the concern, independent air quality monitors have reported significantly higher pollution readings than the government’s official figures, raising questions about the consistency of data.

Chief Minister Rekha Gupta said that anti smog operations, including road misting, dust suppression, and the use of electric heaters at hotspots, are ongoing, however experts and critics argue that local measures alone are insufficient. They are urging stronger coordination with neighbouring states to curb vehicle emissions, roadside dust, and stubble burning, which remain major contributors to the crisis.

Even with the easing of restrictions, Delhi residents continue to battle toxic air as winter sets in, highlighting the urgency for sustained, and regionally aligned, pollution control strategies.
