Suresh Nakhua’s repeated errors in Dhruv Rathee case leave the BJP deeply embarrassed

Published on December 5, 2025

Follow Us:

X Facebook Instagram Threads

A Delhi court on December 4, 2025 fined BJP Mumbai spokesperson Suresh Karamshi Nakhua ₹5,000 for yet again filing a defective affidavit in his defamation suit against YouTuber Dhruv Rathee. The case has dragged on for 17 months, not because of complexity, but because Nakhua and his legal team have submitted faulty documents seven times, prompting the judge to warn that one more mistake could lead to the suit being dismissed on the next hearing, scheduled for 11 March 2026.

The dispute began on 7 July 2024, when Rathee released his video My Reply to Godi YouTubers, accusing the Prime Minister of hosting violent and abusive trolls like Ankit Jain, Suresh Nakhua, and Tajinder Bagga. Offended, Nakhua filed a civil defamation suit in mid-July 2024, leading to summons being issued on 19 July and publicised on 24 July, after which Rathee called the case frivolous.

On 6 August 2024, Rathee sought time to reply, and on 16 August, he moved an application to reject the plaint entirely, resulting in the court instructing Nakhua to pay proper court fees. The fees were paid by 27 August, but his verification affidavit was found defective. On 20 September 2024, Rathee pointed out even more errors under Section 26(2) of the BNSS. In January 2025, a Section 63 certificate filed by Nakhua raised eyebrows because it was dated 26 January, a national holiday. When the court summoned the notary on 1 August 2025, Nakhua’s counsel admitted the date was wrong.

From August to November 2025, six revised affidavits were filed, all rejected for missing stamps, faulty verification, wrong formats, or incomplete annexures. On 4 December 2025, a seventh defective affidavit pushed the court to finally impose costs, remarking that the BJP spokesperson was taking the court for granted.

This entire chain of errors has turned the case into an embarrassment for the BJP. What was meant to be a strong rebuttal to Rathee has instead showcased carelessness, weak preparation, and repeated procedural failures by a party spokesperson. Instead of Rathee being cornered, the BJP leader himself is facing judicial rebukes, social media mockery, and the possibility that his own case may collapse before even reaching the evidence stage.
