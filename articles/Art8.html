Teachers on Streets, Leaders in Luxury, Odisha’s Priorities Exposed

Published on December 12, 2025

Follow Us:

X Facebook Instagram Threads
Thousands of primary school teachers in Odisha are protesting on the streets, demanding better pay and job security. At the same time, MLAs inside the State Assembly have approved a massive salary hike for themselves. This sharp contrast has triggered public anger and raised serious questions about government priorities.

Teachers Protest Outside Assembly

More than 13,000 primary school teachers began a sit in protest on December 5 near the Odisha Assembly in Bhubaneswar. The protest has disrupted classes in nearly 20,000 primary schools, affecting around two to three million students.

Teachers are demanding pay parity with neighbouring states. Their key demands include an upgrade to Pay Level 9, regularisation of contractual teachers, pension benefits, and an end to the six year contractual system for new recruits.

Many teachers say they earn between ₹16,000 and ₹20,000 per month, far lower than teachers in states like Andhra Pradesh and Telangana. Promises made by the government in previous years have not been fulfilled, which has deepened the anger.

The School and Mass Education Minister held talks with teacher representatives but said all demands cannot be met immediately. With no written assurance, the protest continues.

MLAs Approve Big Salary Hike

On December 9, the Odisha Assembly unanimously passed bills increasing MLAs salaries and allowances. The decision was supported by BJP, BJD, Congress and other parties.

MLAs monthly pay has jumped from about ₹1.11 lakh to ₹3.45 lakh. The hike includes higher basic salary and multiple allowances for travel, medical expenses, electricity, phone bills and office work. Pension for former MLAs has also been increased.

The hike is effective from June 2024 and will cost the state exchequer hundreds of crores, including arrears. One MLA, CPI M leader Laxman Munda, did not attend the vote and instead met protesting teachers. He criticised the decision as unjust and insensitive.

Public Anger Grows

The timing of the salary hike has drawn sharp criticism. Many citizens point out that MLAs approved higher pay for themselves while teachers were protesting just outside the Assembly gates.

On social media, people have accused all political parties of coming together only when it comes to personal benefits. Many posts describe the move as greed and betrayal. Comparisons are being made between teachers living in tents and MLAs enjoying luxury.

Several users have demanded that MLA salaries be linked to performance and public service. Videos and posts on the issue have received thousands of likes and shares, reflecting widespread frustration.

A Bigger Question of Priorities

Odisha is facing unemployment, rising prices, farmer distress and shortages in the education sector. Thousands of teaching posts remain vacant, and many trained teachers leave the state for better opportunities elsewhere.

Against this backdrop, the salary hike has deepened public distrust. Critics say investing in teachers and education should come before increasing political perks.

As the teachers protest enters its second week, all eyes are on the government. Whether it chooses dialogue and reform or silence will shape public faith in governance. For a state that speaks of development and a strong future, the treatment of its teachers may prove to be the real test.
