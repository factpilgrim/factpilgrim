BJP Attacks Opposition on Education While Gujarat, Assam and UP Top India’s School Dropout Crisis

Published on December 7, 2025

Follow Us:

X Facebook Instagram Threads

As India faces a shocking increase in school dropouts, fresh government data uncovers a harsh fact: the three states with the most children missing school are all under Bharatiya Janata Party (BJP) control. Despite long BJP rule in these areas, more than 4.9 lakh children lack education this year alone. This happens while BJP leaders often attack opposition-run states for the same problems, showing clear double standards.

A Growing National Emergency

The newest numbers, shared in Parliament by Minister of State for Women and Child Development Savitri Thakur, reveal that India saw 65.7 lakh children drop out of school from 2021-22 to 2025-26. Almost half of them, 29.8 lakh, were teenage girls denied learning chances by poverty, migration, child work, and home tasks. The trend looks dark year after year; estimates from UDISE+ data show in 2021-22 about 27.8 lakh children left, including 12.7 lakh girls; this dropped to 9.8 lakh total in 2022-23 (4.7 lakh girls), then 7.8 lakh in 2023-24 (3.5 lakh girls). But early data for 2024-25 rose to 11.8 lakh (5.1 lakh girls), and 2025-26 holds at 8.5 lakh (3.8 lakh girls). These add to a country-wide issue, with watchers blaming weak buildings and unfair access.

BJP States Top the Dropout List

Leading this rank are BJP strongholds Gujarat, Assam, and Uttar Pradesh, areas where the party has governed for years. Gujarat, the BJP's top base, has had the party in power for about 30 years since first winning in 1995, with steady rule since 1998. Yet it shows 2.4 lakh out-of-school children this year, including 1.1 lakh girls, a huge 341 percent jump from only 54,541 last year. Experts link this to the state's fresh school mergers, which shut small low-student sites, pushing kids from far spots to travel long or stop school. Assam, under BJP watch for nine years since 2016, comes next with 1.5 lakh dropouts, 57,000 of them girls, caused by moves and work in tea fields. Uttar Pradesh, BJP-led for eight years since 2017, counts 99,000 children out of school, with 56,000 girls most hurt by family jobs and money woes.

These lapses shine in places the BJP calls wins. In Gujarat, even with billions in plans like Samagra Shiksha, the dropout rise means money misses the base; rural girls suffer most. Assam's count shows holes in tribal zones, where homes and rides vowed in country programs stay rare. In Uttar Pradesh, the biggest state by people, the BJP's big plans left simple schooling behind, with join-back efforts weak despite many vows.

Misleading Claims Against Non-BJP Areas

This truth makes the BJP's tough hits on non-BJP states feel empty. Just last month, BJP heads hit Delhi's past AAP rule for graft in school builds, saying a 20 percent fail rate in tests and shifted funds worth hundreds of crores. In Karnataka, under Congress for over two years, the party called teacher lacks and a 200 crore laptop issue signs of carelessness. In Kerala, run by the Left for almost a decade, BJP names like Union Minister Dharmendra Pradhan tied 30 percent young job loss to broken schooling, skipping the state's 94 percent read rate.

Voices Demand Real Change

Opposition groups call out the two-faced ways. "The BJP rules the top dropout states for decades, yet points fingers at others," said Congress MP Renuka Chaudhary, who brought up the matter in Parliament. "Their Gujarat way fails our girls; how can they teach Kerala on work?" Former Delhi education minister Atishi added, noting her old team's schools ranked top in CBSE scores while BJP areas trail.

Plans like free clothes and KGBV homes for girls seek to mend this, with 56,700 crore rupees spent last year. But watchers say more counts: better watch via UDISE+ data, blocks on mergers, and true push on girls' blocks. As the new year starts, the query stays: will the BJP face its past or keep the fault-find? For millions of kids, the fix cannot wait.
