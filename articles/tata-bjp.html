Biggest Political Donation Ever Why ₹758 Crore Reached BJP Days Before Voting Began

Published on December 8, 2025

Follow Us

X Facebook Instagram Threads

A major investigation by Scroll reveals that the Tata Group donated ₹758 crore to the BJP on April 2, 2024, just 17 days before voting began in the general election. This is the largest single-day political donation ever recorded in India.

What makes the timing striking is that four weeks earlier, the Union Cabinet approved ₹44,000 crore in subsidies for two Tata semiconductor plants. The Tata Group also donated smaller amounts to the Congress (₹77 crore) and eight regional parties (₹10 crore each), but 83% of its total donation that day went to the BJP.

This information became public only in November 2025 because the donation fell in the next financial year, delaying disclosure. The data is available on the Election Commission website; Scroll's reporter, Aayush Darji, spotted the pattern while tracking political finance.

The report draws parallels to similar cases, such as the Murugappa Group, which also donated shortly after receiving semiconductor subsidies, and past instances like DLF donating to the BJP while its case involving Robert Vadra went cold.

The story raises concerns about possible quid pro quo, though legally proving this is difficult given India's weak regulation of corporate political funding. While the Supreme Court struck down electoral bonds for being opaque, companies can still donate through electoral trusts and direct transfers, which remain legal.

The larger issue highlighted is that India relies on private political funding, unlike many democracies that use public funding. This creates an uneven playing field, especially since earlier analysis showed the BJP receives 58% of all political funding in India.

Scroll argues that major media outlets avoid such stories due to advertising pressures from both corporates and the government. Independent media, therefore, carries the burden of reporting on political finance and threats to election integrity.
