Supreme Court Petition Filed Against Asaram’s Interim Bail by Minor Rape Survivor

Published on December 2, 2025

The minor rape survivor filed a petition in the Supreme Court on December 1, 2025, seeking cancellation of the interim bails granted to Asaram by the Rajasthan and Gujarat High Courts. The plea alleges misuse of medical bail, violation of conditions and continued threat to the survivor’s safety.

Convicted Godman Serving Two Life Sentences

Asaram Bapu was arrested on August 31, 2013, for raping a 16-year-old girl at his Jodhpur ashram. He was convicted on April 25, 2018, and sentenced to life imprisonment. In 2023, he was also convicted in the 2002 Gujarat rape case and given another life term.

Bail Timeline Shows a Pattern of Medical Relief

On January 7, 2024, the Supreme Court granted interim medical bail, later extended until March 31. The Rajasthan High Court extended it further in April 2024 despite allegations of sermons. In August 2024, the Gujarat High Court rejected bail. On November 6, 2025, it granted six-month medical bail. Rajasthan High Court also granted interim bail in October 2025.

Petition Flags Misuse and Violations

The petition states that Asaram travelled between Rishikesh, Maharashtra and other places instead of staying under medical supervision. Videos show him addressing followers, violating bail conditions. His latest medical reports show stable health, contradicting claims of severe illness.

Family Reports Death Threats and Intimidation

The survivor’s father told the court that the family has been receiving threats. He alleged that Asaram’s followers have a history of violence and attempts to silence witnesses, making the interim bail dangerous for the family.

Court History Shows Earlier Concerns

In 2024, the Supreme Court fined Asaram one lakh rupees for submitting misleading medical documents. Judges have stressed that this is a POCSO case that requires strict scrutiny and that bail must be based only on genuine medical grounds.

Widespread Public Outrage on Social Media

This topic trended online, with people questioning how a convict serving two life sentences continues to secure interim bail. Public anger reflects frustration over perceived judicial leniency.

Decision May Shape Medical Bail Rules in POCSO Cases

Legal experts say the Supreme Court’s decision could set a precedent for future medical bail decisions in serious offences involving minors. The case is being compared to the Bilkis Bano remission controversy, raising questions about judicial consistency and victim protection.

The Supreme Court's decision on the survivor's petition will be crucial in determining whether medical bail can continue to serve as an escape route for convicts in serious POCSO cases. It will also signal how firmly the justice system prioritises victim protection over the influence and reach of powerful offenders.
